Our next step was to go deeper into the analysis of the popularity of the restaurants in Toronto. For the same, we used the reviews for doing sentiment analysis and ratings to gauge the popularity of the cuisines and the restaurants. Using a library in Python, we got a sentiment score for each review (a value between -1 and 1), and we used these values to get the average sentiment score for each restaurant. We also used the number of check ins as a measure of the popularity of the restaurants.

For further sentiment analysis, we analysed the sentiment progression over time. In the same time series graph, we plotted the sentiment scores of the reviews of yelp elite users - marked in blue.

Since it was a bit difficult to visualise the progression over time for the entire year as can be seen in the graph above, we visualised the progression only over a period of 6 months as shown below -

We observed that very often, the markers that indicate the review sentiment scores for yelp elite users showed up at the peaks of the time series graph. We also saw that all the scores for the yelp elite users lied within a specific range  - a hypothesis that we drew from that was that the yelp elite users were probably not writing very extreme reviews.
